About ARIL

The Autonomous Robotics Intelligence Lab (ARIL) focuses on building intelligent systems that can perceive, understand, and interact with complex environments.

Our research spans autonomous driving, robotic perception, multi-modal sensing, and edge AI systems. We aim to enable robust decision-making in situations where direct observations are incomplete or unreliable.

Research Direction

We focus on perception under uncertainty, particularly in Non-Line-of-Sight (NLoS) environments where objects are not directly visible.

Our goal is to develop reflection-aware reasoning systems that leverage indirect signals, spatial constraints, and multi-modal data to improve localization and scene understanding.

Research Focus

We investigate how robots can understand environments where visibility is limited. This includes leveraging reflection signals, multi-sensor fusion, and physically grounded reasoning.

Our work integrates vision, radar, lidar, and audio to improve robustness in real-world scenarios such as autonomous driving and urban robotics.
