About ARIL

The Autonomous Robotics Intelligence Lab (ARIL) focuses on building intelligent systems that can perceive, understand, and interact with complex real-world environments. Our research aims to develop robust robotic intelligence for scenarios where observations are incomplete, uncertain, or dynamically changing.

We are particularly interested in autonomous driving, robotic perception, multi-modal sensing, and physically grounded reasoning. By combining machine learning with system-level design, we seek to create autonomous systems that are both reliable and practical for deployment in real-world applications.

Research Direction

Our research direction centers on perception and reasoning under uncertainty. In many real environments, autonomous systems cannot rely solely on direct line-of-sight observations because important information may be partially occluded, distorted, or hidden.

To address this, we study methods that leverage indirect cues, spatial constraints, and multi-modal sensor information to improve robustness in perception and localization. Our goal is to design intelligent robotic systems that can infer hidden structure, reason beyond immediate observations, and make reliable decisions in safety-critical settings.

Research Focus

Autonomous Driving / Robotics Scenario
Multi-modal Perception / NLoS Example
At ARIL, we explore research topics spanning autonomous robotics, intelligent perception, and robust scene understanding. A major focus of our work is how robots can operate effectively in challenging conditions such as occlusion, limited visibility, and Non-Line-of-Sight (NLoS) environments.

We investigate how indirect cues such as reflections, acoustic information, radar responses, and other multi-sensor signals can be integrated with vision-based perception to recover hidden information. Through this, we aim to improve localization, environment understanding, and decision-making in applications such as autonomous driving, urban robotics, and edge AI systems.

In addition, we are interested in real-time deployment, computational efficiency, and system optimization so that our models can operate robustly on practical robotic platforms.
